# Zepto-Inventory-Analysis
This dataset provides a detailed view of the product catalog and pricing structure of Zepto, a fast-growing 10-minute grocery delivery platform. The data captures essential attributes for over 3,000+ SKUs (Stock Keeping Units) across various categories like Fruits & Vegetables, Dairy, Packaged Foods, Beverages, and more.

The data is structured to support various types of retail analysis, including:
1. Discount trends by category
2. Inventory availability and stock-outs
3. Price distribution and pricing strategy
4. Product naming patterns (suitable for word cloud or NLP tasks)

**Key takeaways:**
Category‑specific discounting: Instead of blanket promotions, tailor discounts to categories where price sensitivity is highest. This prevents margin erosion in premium segments while still driving volume in competitive ones.

Premium product focus: For high‑end SKUs, availability and reliability matter more than discounts. Customers buying premium dairy or imported foods value consistency, so stock management is the lever here.

Bulk product advantage: Larger pack sizes or bundled items deliver better perceived value, increase basket size, and reduce delivery frequency. This is a win for both customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

Inventory optimization: Balancing stock levels is critical. Over‑stocking ties up capital and risks waste, while under‑stocking leads to missed sales and poor customer experience. Smart forecasting and replenishment are key to growth and cost control.

Together, these findings suggest a segmented retail strategy: discounts where they matter, availability where it counts, and inventory discipline across the board. That’s exactly the kind of nuanced approach quick‑commerce players like Zepto need to sustain growth while protecting margins.
